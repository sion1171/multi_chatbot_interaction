Title of study

Restructuring Human-AI Communication Dynamics: The Potential of Multi-Chatbot Persuasion in Mediated Communication in a Charitable Giving Context


Investigator

Yoo Jung (Erika) Oh, Diane Kim, Muhammad Rasul

Introduction and Purpose

You are being invited to join a research study. The purpose of this study is to test the acceptability and usability of children’s charity chatbots.

If you agree to be in this research, you will be asked to complete two sets of surveys including pre-study questionnaire and post-study questionnaire, and engage in an online text-based interaction with a children’s charity chatbots. You will be asked questions about your sociodemographic information, chatbot usability, perceptions towards the chatbot, perceptions towards the conversation and/or messages, and charity donation intentions. It will take about 30 minutes to complete questionnaires and engage with chatbots.

Porential Benefits

You may not benefit personally from being in this study.

Potential Risks

There are no foreseeable risks to participating in this study.

Privacy and Confidentiality

The PI and Co-PIs will have password-protected access to the data and they will be stored indefinitely. We will not store identifiable information. Summary of the data may be presented in a conferenced and published in academic journals.

Your rights to participate, say no, or withdraw

You have the right to say no to participate in the research. You can stop at any time after it has already started. There will be no consequences if you stop and you will not be criticized. You will not lose any benefits that you normally receive.

Costs and Compensation for being in the study

For study compensation, you will receive extra credit.

Questions

If you have any questions about this research, please feel free to contact the investigator at ohyoojun@msu.edu
